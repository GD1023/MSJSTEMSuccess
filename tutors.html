For tutors

Teach the course you just mastered.

Tutor applications are accepted on a rolling basis. You can tutor any math or science course you've completed with a good grade( -A or above) or teacher recommendation.

Qualifications

What we look for.

We expect our tutors to bring more than just a good grade — patience and consistency matter just as much.

  • A demonstrated passion for math and science.
  • Experience and patience tutoring others.
  • A B-minimum grade in all math and science courses.
  • Willingness to commit at least 2 hours a week.

Duties of an active tutor

Once you're matched, here's the standard.

01

Meet twice a month

Tutor each tutee at least two times a month, one hour per session, in an MSJ classroom.

02

One Advisory event

Tutor at least once for an Advisory Special Event each semester.

03

Follow the role guide

Active tutors are expected to follow the program's Tutor Role & Expectations document in full.

The leadership team reserves the right to remove a tutor's role if expectations aren't met.

Benefits

What active tutors get back.

  • Service hours for every session you tutor.
  • Special consideration for next year's leadership positions.
  • A real line for your resume — "Peer Tutor, MSJ STEM Success."
  • Direct updates from the Leadership Team on events and program news.

Scheduling

Same classrooms, same supervision.

Like student sessions, tutoring happens in MSJ classrooms under teacher supervision, after school or during Advisory.
